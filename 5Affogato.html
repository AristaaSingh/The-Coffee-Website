The Caffè Affogato


An affogato, more traditionally known as "affogato al caffè" (Italian for "drowned in coffee"), is an Italian coffee-based dessert. It usually takes the form of a scoop of plain milk-flavored (fior di latte) or vanilla gelato or ice cream topped or "drowned" with a shot of hot espresso. Some variations also include a shot of amaretto, Bicerin, Kahlúa, or other liqueur. Whether a dessert or beverage, restaurants and cafes usually serve the affogato in a tall narrowing glass, allowing the fior di latte, vanilla gelato, or ice cream to melt and combine with espresso into the hollowed space in the bottom of the glass.(1)


Make the Perfect Caffè Affogato!

  1. Brew the espresso. Using your desired brewing method, brew 1 shot espresso.

  2. Scoop the ice cream. As the espresso is brewing, drop 2 scoops vanilla gelato or ice cream into a small drinking glass.

  3. Add the espresso and amaretto. Pour the espresso shot and 1 splash amaretto over the ice cream.

  4. Garnish and serve. Top with shaved dark chocolate and serve immediately. Your Affogato is ready! (2)
